Kerala

Known as "God's Own Country," Kerala boasts palm-fringed beaches, tranquil backwaters, and lush tea plantations. Visitors immerse themselves in Ayurvedic treatments, houseboat cruises along serene canals, and cultural experiences like Kathakali performances, all amidst the region's serene natural beauty.

Manali

Nestled in the Himalayas, Manali is a serene hill station renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, adventure sports, and vibrant culture. Visitors flock here for skiing in winter, trekking in summer, and exploring the quaint streets lined with charming cafes and handicraft shops.

Goa

Goa is India's party capital, renowned for its sandy beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese heritage. Travelers revel in water sports, beach parties, and exploring colonial architecture and spice plantations. Goa offers a unique blend of relaxation and excitement against a backdrop of swaying palms and azure waters.

Pondicherry

Pondicherry, or Puducherry, exudes a quaint French charm with its colonial architecture, cobblestone streets, and tranquil beaches. Visitors explore the vibrant French Quarter, indulge in delectable cuisine at chic cafes, and unwind with yoga and meditation by the sea, all in a laid-back, cosmopolitan atmosphere.

Price: $200
Book now

Coorg

Often called the "Scotland of India," Coorg is a lush green district in Karnataka famed for its misty hills, coffee plantations, and rich biodiversity. Travelers indulge in activities like hiking through dense forests, sipping freshly brewed coffee, and unwinding amidst picturesque valleys.

Price: $200
Book now

Araku

Tucked away in the Eastern Ghats, Araku Valley is a hidden gem offering stunning landscapes, tribal culture, and aromatic coffee plantations. Travelers embark on scenic train rides through verdant valleys, trek to cascading waterfalls, and immerse themselves in the rich tribal heritage of the region.
